still being on EST has its upsides

Oddly, if you get up nice and early, like before the sun is screaming down from the sky, bombing around in the desert is not a completely miserable endeavor. We took advantage of this fact and pointed the Jeep towards Valley of Fire State park in Overton. With the top down, a pack of donuts and Slim Jims in tow, we made quick work of the drive (fighting off urges to stop at rest area taco trucks) and managed to arrive shortly before 9am.

Some of the sites called out by the park were awe inspiring (being enveloped in a petroglyph cover canyon with a much appreciated cool breeze) others markedly less so (1/2 mile hike from the road to see a single petrified log surrounding by a huge fence). In all cases, it was a little toasty. After a few hours of driving/hiking/sweating we stopped in the gift shop to freshen up and started out way back to the strip. As we were leaving around 1pm it was 112° and it looked like the bulk of visitors were just starting to pile in for the day. I really hope they brought plenty of water and giant blocks of dry ice.

Pool. Nap.

Despite knowing full well that the city is host to some of the finest dining options available for purchase, we had an overwhelming desire to see an old friend the buffet at the Bellagio. We had been there in the past with Erik and Amanda, and it seemed like gorging ourselves on food that is really quite fantastic, was the proper way to bring a sense of family into the trip.

By a stroke of serendipitous scheduling, our trip overlapped with Camp Organic, so we were lucky enough to drag Mr. Sam Cannon away from his duties as counselor for a few moments. Taking full advantage of the situation we scampered over to what can best be described as a 1973 Denny's, done vegas-style, serving booze, with sunken seating and flaming fountains, turned up to 11: The Peppermill. After soaking in our glorious surroundings and a round of celebratory shameful looking drinks we returned Sam to his duties and knowing we had a long travel day ahead of us, called it a night.
