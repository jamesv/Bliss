our transition back to normal life will be difficult

As it turns out, renting a car from a place specializing in exotic imports, in a stripmall, is preciously as sketchy as you would expect it to be. While we were waiting to check out, we were repeatedly offered an adorable puppy in exchange for paying off a few bills for a couple that had landed on hard times. Luckily for us, the owner of an adjoining shop had a soft spot for pits and gave Chico a new home. We collected our Wrangler and set about our grand tour of the strip, ending with a rambling stroll through the Palazzo Shops @ The Venetian.

We returned to our room, set down our things and were startled by a knock at the door. Having a reasonable degree of confidence that we were not about to be thrown out, James answered and was presented with a lovely bottle of wine from his mother. Thank you Angie & Ed! It was opened we went to work on it almost immediately. Then took a nap.

Afterwards we laid around the pool until we were kicked out at closing.

Needing sustenance after roasting for hours, our thoughts turned to an Indian restaurant uncovered in previous night's buffet hunt. We fired up the GPS and bolted away from the sights and sounds of the strip, ending up at a tiny building in the parking lot of a Staples. Upon entering we found ourselves the sole patrons and I think we even startled the man behind the register who was deeply involved in the Bengaluru soap opera blaring on a flatscreen by the kitchen. This was the right place to be. We placed our order and the place slowly began to fill in with Punjabi locals, another excellent sign. The food was incredible and we spent the remainder of the evening driving around just taking in the night air.
